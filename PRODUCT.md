# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Tanaghum serves commercial, marketing, sales, content, review, and executive roles inside customer organizations. Users are usually completing repeated daily work under time pressure: planning offers and events, creating content, approving work, following leads, and reading performance. Administrators configure people, security, integrations, and tenant policy without exposing technical infrastructure to normal users.

## Product Purpose

Tanaghum is a governed commercial operating system. It connects planning, event operations, AI-assisted content, approvals, scheduling, CRM follow-up, and trusted reporting in one tenant-isolated workflow. Success means a non-technical manager can understand the current situation, identify the next action, complete it safely, and verify the result without learning the platform's internal architecture.

## Brand Personality

Clear, capable, and calm. Tanaghum should feel credible enough for executives, efficient enough for daily operators, and helpful without becoming decorative or theatrical.

## Anti-references

- Engineering consoles presented as customer products.
- Marketing landing pages used as operational dashboards.
- Long walls of cards, pills, warnings, or instructional copy.
- Navigation organized around backend modules or internal acronyms.
- Oversized dark heroes, gradients, glass panels, and decorative motion on routine work surfaces.
- Interfaces that expose demo, sprint, mock, runtime bridge, MCP, SAIF, STITCH, or M5 language to customer roles.

## Design Principles

1. Start with today's job. Every screen makes one primary action obvious before secondary context.
2. Follow the business journey. Navigation and state use Plan, Create, Review, Schedule, Follow Up, and Measure language.
3. Reveal complexity by permission and need. Setup, administration, diagnostics, and governance remain available without competing with daily work.
4. Keep AI governed and visible. Stitchi prepares useful work, asks for missing information, shows its proposal, and executes internal changes only after approval.
5. Prefer operational truth over visual noise. Empty, blocked, and credential-dependent states explain the next real step without invented data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All core workflows must support keyboard navigation, visible focus, semantic controls, 44px touch targets, reduced motion, readable contrast, zoom, long content, and responsive behavior from 390px mobile through wide desktop. Customer-facing copy must be plain enough for non-technical roles and structurally ready for future Arabic and RTL support.
