# Commercial/Social Product Storyboard

## Ten-Minute Customer Flow

### 1. Marketing Manager Opens Command Center

The first screen shows a Commercial/Social operating workspace. The manager sees the active campaign, today’s next action, approval pressure, publishing readiness, performance signal, and qualified leads.

The environment indicator is small: Sandbox Workspace, External Writes Off, Scheduling Disabled.

### 2. Active Campaign And Next Action

The manager sees the active campaign title, objective, audience, platforms, and current workflow state. A single next-action panel tells them whether to generate drafts, score content, submit approval, or prepare publishing.

### 3. Campaign Workspace

The manager opens Campaigns and reviews:

- Objective.
- Audience.
- Platforms.
- Tone.
- CTA.
- Offer or content angle.

The page feels like campaign work, not system setup.

### 4. AI Draft Generation

The manager clicks Generate Platform Drafts. The system creates LinkedIn, Instagram, and X/Twitter drafts through the backend AI provider path.

The user sees editable platform drafts and selects one.

### 5. Score And Optimize

The selected draft is scored. The manager sees:

- Overall readiness score.
- Best platform.
- Best posting time.
- Best format.
- Hook quality.
- CTA strength.
- Hashtag hygiene.
- Risk/compliance notes.

### 6. Submit For Approval

The manager sends the selected draft for approval. The page shows Approval Required and moves the workflow forward.

### 7. Approver Approves

The approver opens Approvals & Publishing, reviews the pending package, and approves, rejects, or requests changes. Approval unlocks publishing preparation.

### 8. Publishing Package Becomes Ready

After approval, the publishing package shows:

- Platform payloads.
- Prepared schedule window.
- Postiz payload summary.
- Scheduling disabled reason.
- Required authorization state.

No live publishing happens by default.

### 9. Analytics Shows Performance Intelligence

Analytics & Leads shows performance intelligence for the campaign:

- Reach.
- Impressions.
- Engagement.
- Best platform.
- Best time.
- Learning signal.

The copy explains that official read-only APIs can replace sandbox source after credentials and authorization.

### 10. Leads Show Qualified Opportunities

The manager reviews realistic qualified opportunities:

- LinkedIn Lead - Enterprise Training Inquiry.
- Instagram Lead - Course Follow-up Request.
- X Lead - Partnership Information Request.

Each record includes source, campaign attribution, intent, qualification score, owner, current stage, payload status, and next action.

### 11. GHL And Voice/Chat Handoff

The manager previews:

- GHL contact payload.
- GHL opportunity payload.
- Voice/chat follow-up package.
- Suggested script/intent.
- Consent/status fields.

Actions remain gated with customer-facing reasons: Requires Credentials, Requires Authorization, Sandbox Scheduling Disabled.

### 12. Evidence Behind Secondary Access

Audit and evidence exist, but they do not dominate the customer walkthrough. The customer can access Evidence / Audit from Admin / Technical if needed.

## Customer Message

This is a working Commercial/Social AI operating module. It helps the marketing team prepare campaign content, adapt it for each platform, score and optimize it, route human approval, prepare Postiz scheduling payloads, understand performance, qualify leads, and prepare CRM or voice/chat handoff while unsafe execution stays governed.

## Success Signal

The customer understands the product without needing an architecture explanation and can follow the workflow from campaign to lead handoff in one coherent path.
